EUDI WALLET · UX DIRECTION · issuance-first

The picker chooses the wallet.
You own everything after the handoff.

With the Digital Credentials API, presentation is becoming an OS-mediated moment. So the flow that decides whether people trust, keep, and use this wallet is issuance — getting a credential in.

OS owns

Selection. Which wallet answers a request — the cross-wallet chooser (Android Credential Manager, Safari sheet).

You own

Issuance. The OpenID4VCI provisioning flow after hand-off — still app-driven, most failure-prone.

You own

Consent. The what/who/why screen the wallet renders once the picker hands control back.

You own

Trust surfaces. Privacy dashboard, over-ask warnings, recovery — the picker can't offer these.

Meet the offer where it lives — issuer first

Trust is anchored in the government issuer, not the wallet vendor — the pattern every platform wallet uses.

STEP 02 · expectations

Name the hard step before it happens

Surface the card + PIN requirement — and a forgotten-PIN path — before the tap, not after a failure. Progressive disclosure: the highest-friction step comes only after value is shown.

Front-loaded friction is a documented conversion killer; a mid-flow “where’s my card / PIN?” is a silent drop.

STEP 03 · the make-or-break moment

Obsess over the NFC tap + PIN

Device-specific placement animation, live “reading… keep still” feedback, auto-resume on a slip (never a full restart), and remaining-attempts on the system PIN sheet. The wallet never sees the PIN.

Germany’s PID rides the national eID over NFC (Ausweis SDK). This single screen decides onboarding — see the full state + recovery spec in the companion doc.

STEP 04 · consent to issue

Show exactly what’s being written, from whom

Legibility is the core trust mechanic; the reference wallet’s raw attribute checklist is the anti-pattern.

STEP 05 · async, made friendly

Deferred issuance is a state, not a spinner

Real issuers deliver asynchronously. Say so warmly, push on completion, offer “check status”, and give a “didn’t appear?” recovery — and disambiguate “the ID you log in with” from “the credential stored.”

The exact confusion + silent “empty section” that stalled Italy’s IT-Wallet users.

STEP 06 · convert to an active wallet

End on what they can do — then a first real use

The success screen leads straight into a concrete, privacy-preserving action, and offers the next credential. Trigger the whole activation at this moment of success, never from a settings menu.

A post-success prompt is the strongest known conversion lever; the over-18-without-details framing is the single most persuasive adoption hook.

STEP 07 · presentation, thin but yours

The OS picks the wallet — the consent screen is still yours

Keep presentation a thin, standards-conformant layer, but make the post-hand-off consent excellent: who / what / why, an explicit “not shared” line, and an over-ask warning. Guide the choice; don’t just list attributes.

A guided “credential assistant” roughly halved over-disclosure; a passive checklist doesn’t. The over-ask warning is a safety moment platform pickers structurally can’t offer.

Beyond the flow

Where a government wallet out-designs the platform

Trust surfaces the picker can’t own

  • “Who has my data & why” feed — the eIDAS transaction log as a friendly activity view, with one-tap stop-sharing + GDPR erasure.
  • Over-ask warnings + Trust Mark — visible safety and certification cues.
  • No lock-in — wallet-to-wallet portability, marketed as a promise, not a setting.
  • Assured recovery — re-proof at the original assurance level (NFC + liveness), never a phishable SMS fallback.

The bet, stated plainly

  • Issuance is the flagship — hardest, most failure-prone, most fully wallet-owned surface.
  • PID is the never-empty base — solve cold-start the way Apple’s passport ID and Italy’s IO app did.
  • Keep OID4VP + proximity as defaults — Safari has no OpenID4VP, BLE bypasses the API, the standard is still a Working Draft.
  • Acceptance > interface — ship every credential with a named place to use it today.
